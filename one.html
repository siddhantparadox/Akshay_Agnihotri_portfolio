Picxy Case Study

About the Client:
Picxy Digital Services Pvt Ltd is a leading South Asia's provider of high-quality licensed photographs and videos to businesses, marketing agencies and media organizations around the world. Working with its growing community of over 10,000 contributors, Picxy adds thousands of images each month and currently has more than a million images.
Team:
Buyer side team and Contributor side team.
Role:
UX/UI Designer.
Challenges:
When I joined Picxy, Picxy was 2 years old. It was built rapidly in order to meet the needs of the users. As an UX/UI designer, I worked with both buyer side team and contributor side team. Our challenges were to:
• Define missing UX fundamentals.
• Correct existing UX issues.
• Continue to build out new features and content.
Challenge #1:
Define UX Fundamentals
When I came on, the team had done admirable work in vetting features, but no core persona work had been done. Picxy is for buyer side and contributor side. How do you design, without understanding the needs of each? Seeing this lack, I made it my mission to fill in these details.
Solution:
• I advocated up the chain for a persona development.
This included stakeholder interviews with my CEO, Business development head, and Contributor side team head, since they knew more about the existing customer segments and users.
Challenge #2:
Correct existing UX issues
I started off by doing a heuristic analysis and cognitive walkthrough of the website. This was done to uncover the usability issues. Also I Conducted multiple interview sessions with stakeholders who directly communicated with the customers to understand the problems they are facing on the current website.
Problems that were discovered:
• The customers were not sure about how the pricing works.
• The checkout process had a lot of redundant steps.
Solution:
I got to know that there was a business need to change the pricing strategy and I was working on the redesign of pricing page, so, to solve both the problems I facilitated a brainstorming workshop with stakeholders.
Then, I did a competitive analysis. It was especially important – stock photo companies tend use very similar page layouts, and buyers usually compare many websites, so they come to expect particular design patterns.
I did some sketching to explore layouts and generate some ideas and validated it with stakeholders.

Two cases were considered:
• One time buying flow.
• Subscription plan or value pack buying flow.
I read about the best practices for checkout page that some good UX practitioners use. Also, went to different sites to explore different checkout processes, all of this gave me a good insight.

Outcomes:
By making all the changes and the redesigns, we saw a rise in user buying more value packs and also the users opting for subscription plans (recurring payment) saw a rise. The revenue of the company increased by making all the changes, this shows that the decisions that we took were right.
Challenge #3:
Design contributor home page
There was no home page for the contributors side to show the benefits of selling their content through Picxy. So, we decided to design home page for contributors side.
In a competitive analysis, I looked at other websites that customers are likely to encounter and use. The idea was to get an idea on how the content was presented and what patterns were used to do it.
I facilitated a remote workshop with contributor side stakeholders, to understand the needs of contributors. We also brainstormed on the content strategy for the home page. We came up with quick sketches to make our idea concrete.
I created wireframes, to determine the information hierarchy of the design while making it easier to plan out the content and user experience.

By making the home page for contributor side, we saw an increase in engagement on critical CTAs, increase in engagement with navigational elements and increase in signups. We saw increase in incoming photos as well which helped business to increase the photo repository.
Other Notable Work:
There were two challenges and they were to redesign contributor dashboard and design contributor app.
The goals were:
• The first version of contributor dashboard was built quickly before I came to Picxy. In the haste, the UX stumbled in places. I took the responsibility of smoothing out these experiences.
• For contributor app, I built a MVP contributors app to increase the inflow of the content and to pitch it to investors as a product roadmap strategy.
Segregate/Categorize the content
In heuristic evaluation, I found out that the search needs to be seriously worked on. As, I reported this to all the stakeholders, we decided that we need a temporary solution on this because changing search algorithm would take a lot of time.
So, for temporary solution we decided that we should segregate/categorize the content, I conducted a workshop with stakeholders, to brainstorm on the idea of segregation, and we came up with a strategy to divided the single photo page into 4 categories namely, feature stock images, trending images, Indian faces and Picxy original.
By doing the segregation, we wanted to add the sections of segregated pages on the home page.
The stakeholders wanted a fresh view of the Picxy's home page and at the same time usability should not take a toll, so all the best practices of home pages was taken into consideration to make the home page usable as well.
