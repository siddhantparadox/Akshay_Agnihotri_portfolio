LightMetrics Case Study

About the Client:
LightMetrics provide video telematics and real-time video feedback solutions to fleet customers ranging from heavy utility trucks to LUVs. Solution consists of camcorder and SDK that analyzes video in real-time.
Brief:
We were asked to redesign the website that curates current, credible, reliable content for users which helps to generate business leads.
Research:
The first step in the project was to learn about the existing website and it's problems, so to do that I carried out cognitive walkthrough. Here are some of the findings that were obtained:
• The existing website failed to convey what the company is about.
• The website was not structured properly.
• Information didn't feel connected and logical.
• The existing site was full of technical terms.
• A lot of usability issues were found.
In the next step, to confirm findings from the cognitive walkthrough, I decided to send out UX questionnaires to client and their existing customers.
The answers received from the UX questionnaire confirmed the findings from cognitive walkthrough. Here are some of the additional questions that got answered from UX questionnaires:
• What is the value proposition of the product?
• How many products do they offer?
• Who are the customers?
• What competitive advantage do they offer?
• What is the vision and mission of the company?
Problem and the Solution:
During the research, I identified the key user problems that needed to be solved - the users wanted easy navigation and lucid content and language, from the stakeholder interview I got to know there are three main line of products that LightMetrics has to offer and there is a need of careers section on the website.
Persona:
To get a feel of what information do the users seek, I synthesized two personas from stakeholder interviews and competitive analysis. To create a thorough persona I normally include needs, desires, goals and motivations.
Information Architecture:
With the help of all the insights gathered from previous steps, I produced an Information architecture that helped in visualizing site navigation and content language that users would need to achieve their goals.
Exploring the screen:
After creating information architecture, I created low fidelity wireframes that help us put all the necessary information and flow without any visual design. The wireframes were validated with the stakeholders before proceeding to the detail design stage.
For visual design and easier development cycle, we decided to go ahead with WordPress theme. I provided the development team with assets like icons and images.
Outcomes:
After the launch of the redesigned website and having the website under observation for 1 month's time, analytics revealed the following:
• Healthy & steady traffic growth since launch.
• Low overall bounce rate & healthy average time on site indicates good visitor retention.
• Responsive design success revealed in low mobile traffic bounce rate.
• Candidates started applying for the job openings.
Learnings:
• One of my biggest learnings was how to collaborate with stakeholders to make the project a success.
• The views of the stakeholder were very important in shaping the website. They have better knowledge of the company's vision and the user base and getting feedback from them was a crucial step before proceeding further.
• I learnt a lot while collaborating with the developer. I learnt the proper way of preparing assets for developer hand-off.
