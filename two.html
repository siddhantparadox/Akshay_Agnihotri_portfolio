Gigsasa Case Study

About the Client:
Gigsasa is a HR Technology company focused on gig economy by staffing and managing core workforce in construction, hospitality, manufacturing and logistics businesses.
Brief:
Gigsasa aims to achieve two goals which is to connect workers with potential employers and to enable employers to find the right workers to get the job done.
Research:
The first step in the project was to learn more about the gig economy HR technology space so I did a competitive analysis of gig economy competitor companies. After understanding gig economy space, I turned my attention to the target users. For this, I conducted stakeholder interview, in the conversation, I got to know that they did a research of their own. Through the research, we found that the user pain points consisted of:
Workers pain points:
• Taxing to find a job offline.
• Can't afford to pay the agencies
• After the job is done it's hard to get money instantly
• Every time has to check with the agency to change the shift time.
Employers pain points:
• Taxing to find the right candidate.
• Difficulty in handling the transactions.
• Giving feedback is a challenge.
Problem and the Solution:
With the help of research, we identified the key user problems we wanted to solve - workers wanted to find a job with ease and should be flexible but they couldn’t due to the agency culture and the employers wanted to find the right worker with right skill set to get the job done.

So we wanted to provide them with a modern platform where workers can find gig jobs with the help of an app and the employers can appoint the workers with the help of dashboard.
Site Map:
Before diving deep into specific designs, I created site map to help us visualize the main screens and features.
Exploring the screen:
I created user flows based on a model suggested by Ryan Singer, which suggests an approach in which "what the user sees" and "what they do" is taken into consideration and also "what they see next" and "what they do next" is also considered and a user flow is created.
I created wireframes to specifically focus on allocation and prioritization of content functionalities available, and intended behaviours, it allowed us to determine the information hierarchy of the design while making it easier to plan out the content and user experience.

Design Handoff:
Once our designs had matured enough, I used Adobe XD to handoff the designs to the dev team. I also Adobe XD to create interactive prototypes for the screens so the dev teams could understand the interactions in the app and dashboard better.
Quick and Dirty Usability Test:
Due to the project timeline and budget, I ran usability testing with my colleagues. I wanted to do concept validation and discover issues. I ran the test sessions using interactive prototypes and then updated designs based on the feedback I received during usability testing and design reviews.
Outcomes:
After validating everything with stakeholders, I did design handoff to the dev team. Since I left the company for which I was handling the project, I am not aware of any further progress on this project.
Learnings:
• Design critiques helped me to think objectively without getting attached to a particular design.
• Last but not the least thing I learned was a genuine interest in the work you’re doing will be the biggest motivation. Since the stakeholders were from London, I had to work at odd hours to accommodate the time difference but my passion in this project kept me motivated so that I didn’t mind that schedule at all.
